Why this order works: Legs on Monday gives your lower body the maximum possible recovery window before Friday's Hyrox — 4 full days. Push on Tuesday hits opposing muscles to Monday's session, so there's zero overlap. Crossfit on Wednesday acts as a buffer between Push and Pull, with upper-body pressing scaled if needed. Pull on Thursday means your back and biceps are completely fresh going into Hyrox. The two rest days bookend Hyrox for full recovery before the new training week.

Progression model
Double progression
Work within the listed rep range. Once you hit the top of the range across all sets, add weight next session and start at the bottom again. E.g. 3×10 at target → 3×10, 3×11, 3×12 → add 2.5kg → 3×10 again.
RIR targets
Compounds: finish at 1–2 RIR (could do 1–2 more reps if forced). Isolations: 0–1 RIR — go close to failure. This protects your CNS on top of Hyrox and Crossfit volume.
Deload every 6–8 weeks
Drop to 50% of working weight, same rep ranges, for one full week. Non-negotiable at 5 sessions/week. This is when adaptation happens, not during hard training weeks.
Body comp check-in
Weigh daily, use a 7-day rolling average. Dropping more than 0.8kg/week → add 150 kcal (muscle loss risk). Not moving after 2 weeks → drop 100 kcal. Your 2,200–2,400 kcal range is well-calibrated for body recomp.

Recovery
framework

Sleep · nutrition · tools · weekly rhythm

Daily non-negotiables
🌙 Sleep highest priority
7.5–9 hours. More than any supplement or protocol — sleep is where muscle protein synthesis peaks, cortisol resets, and fat oxidation runs. At 5 sessions/week, sub-7 hours will visibly stall body composition progress within 2–3 weeks.
💧 Hydration highest priority
Your existing hydration framework applies. On Hyrox and Crossfit days add 500–750ml beyond your baseline — sweat losses are significantly higher than PPL weight training alone.
Saturday — active recovery (post-Hyrox)
🚶 Low-intensity movement recommended
20–30 min easy walk outdoors. Gentle movement increases blood flow to sore tissue and clears inflammatory markers faster than complete rest. No intensity, no targets, no HR zone.
🧘 Extended mobility session recommended
10–15 min on wherever you feel tightest — usually hips, hamstrings, and upper back after Hyrox. One day per week to do longer holds (2–3 min) rather than the 45–90s used in cool-downs.
Sunday — full rest + batch prep
🍳 Batch cook session highest priority
Preparing food in advance removes decision fatigue on hard training days. When you're tired post-Legs or post-Hyrox, having protein already prepped is what keeps you hitting 178g/day consistently. Chicken, turkey mince, quinoa, overnight oats jars.
Tools and techniques by priority
highCold shower post-Hyrox
2–3 min cold exposure after Friday's session. Reduces systemic inflammation and accelerates recovery. Not after PPL — cold blunts the hypertrophy signal after weight training.
highFoam rolling
5–10 min on training days, focused on muscles just worked. Quads and IT band after Legs, thoracic spine after Push, lats and upper traps after Pull. Slow passes only.
mediumContrast shower
Alternate 1 min hot / 30s cold × 3 cycles, ending cold. Good on Legs and Hyrox days. Drives blood flow in and out of muscle tissue for faster recovery.
mediumMagnesium glycinate
300–400mg before bed on training days. Supports muscle relaxation, sleep quality, and reduces nighttime cramping — especially relevant at your training frequency.
optionalCompression gear
Compression tights Friday evening and Saturday after Hyrox. Reduces fluid pooling in legs overnight and can meaningfully reduce perceived soreness going into the new week.
optionalSauna
15–20 min at 80–90°C on Saturday if accessible. Heat exposure increases growth hormone and supports cardiovascular adaptation. Rest days only — not immediately post-training.
